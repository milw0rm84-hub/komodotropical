Holiday in Paradise Island: 5 Secrets of Komodo Island That Must Be Known

June 01, 2025

Home to the Rarest Creatures on Earth
True to its name, Komodo Island is the native habitat of the species known by its Latin name Varanus Komodoensis, commonly known as the Komodo dragon. This giant lizard is on the list of ancient animals. Uniquely, Komodo dragons cannot be found on any other island in the world except Komodo Island. Despite occasional relocation to other islands, these reptiles seem to have a homesick nature and will return to the island of their birth by swimming. Currently, Komodos are considered rare creatures in the world. It is estimated that the population of this ancient species is only in the thousands.

Abundance of Exotic Wildlife
Despite being named Komodo Island, this beautiful island is home to a variety of exotic wildlife, including diverse mammals, birds, and various reptile species. When you visit Komodo Island, you'll encounter hundreds of local deer inhabiting the island. This moment might make you feel like you're on the African savannah surrounded by grasslands.

A Wonder of the World
As mentioned earlier, Komodo Island is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site since 1991. This recognition is bestowed upon the island due to its rare phenomena and aesthetic qualities.

Interacting with Komodos
Unlike seeing Komodos in zoos or on television, on Komodo Island, you can witness or even directly interact with these colossal reptiles. However, it's crucial to maintain a safe distance of about 300 meters from Komodos. This is because these giant lizards are dangerous wild animals that can attack if they feel threatened. The Komodo's saliva is also highly toxic, containing deadly bacteria that can quickly immobilize its prey. Additionally, Komodos possess a tail swipe that is equally hazardous. As adults, they can reach sizes of up to 3 meters, weigh up to 90 kilograms, and detect their prey from a distance of 12 kilometers. Despite their large size, Komodos are agile creatures with high endurance, enabling them to move much farther than other lizards.

Diving Paradise
Beyond the breathtaking landscapes on land, Komodo Island holds beauty beneath its waters. Situated at the heart of the Asia-Pacific coral triangle, the island boasts hundreds of beautiful coral reefs underwater. There are at least 100 world-class diving locations waiting to be explored around Komodo Island. Besides treating your eyes to underwater beauty, you can also witness large manta rays, whale sharks, and dugongs.
